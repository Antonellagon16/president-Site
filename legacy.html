Legacy of William McKinley

Economic Impact

McKinley's presidency saw strong economic growth. His support for the Gold Standard Act of 1900 provided stability to the U.S. economy. He also championed high tariffs to protect American industries.

Foreign Policy

Under McKinley, the U.S. became a global power. The Spanish-American War resulted in U.S. expansion, acquiring territories such as Puerto Rico, Guam, and the Philippines.
